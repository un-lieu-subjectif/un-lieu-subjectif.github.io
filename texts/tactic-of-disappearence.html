Binary

in 1937, Claude Shannon proved that it was possible to use arrangements of electrical switches (Open/Close, True/False) to solve logical problems. That binary system is the basic concept that underlies all electronic digital computers.

Bit

Binary digits, usually denoted by 0 and 1, is the basic unit of information in computing and telecommunications. 2 bits has four possible states: 00, 01, 10 et 11. 3 bits has height possible states: 000, 001, 010, 011, 100, 101, 110 et 111. Decimal equivalent is 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 et 8

Ba gua - 3000 BC

The ba gua are eight diagrams represent the fundamental principles of reality - seen as divinities, as interrelated concepts. Each consists of three lines, each line either "broken" or "unbroken", yin or yang.

Logos

Originally a word meaning "word," "speech," "account," or "reason,". It became a technical term in philosophy used for the principle of order and knowledge.

Hierarchy

A hierarchy (from hierarches, "leader of sacred rites") is an arrangement of items (objects, names, values, categories, etc.) in which the items are represented as being "above," "below," or "at the same level as" one another.

Internet and Hypertext

The Internet is a global system of interconnected computer networks that use the standard Internet Protocol Suite (TCP/IP) to serve billions of users worldwide. It is a network of networks that consists of millions of private, public, academic, business, and government networks, of local to global scope, that are linked by a broad array of electronic, wireless and optical networking technologies.

Hypertext is text displayed on a computer or other electronic device with references (hyperlinks) to other text that the reader can immediately access. Hypertext is the underlying concept defining the structure of the World Wide Web, making it an easy-to-use and flexible format to share information over the Internet.

Panopticon

Type of prison building designed by English philosopher and social theorist Jeremy Bentham in 1785. The panopticon allow an observer to observe (-opticon) all (pan-) prisoners without the incarcerated being able to tell whether they are being watched.
